MIT App Inventor Project

Duration - September 18, 2018 - October 6, 2018
Course - Computer Science Principles
Objective - Implement new features to a certain game using MIT app inventor
Type of Project - 2 Person Group

For this project, the game that our group worked on was Mole Mash. In the original, unedited version, a mole pops up at a random position on the screen and the player scores points by hitting the mole before it jumps away. My partner and I explored and added bonus features to make the game more intriguing such as a life system, levels, timers, start/instructions screen, a cloud saving score, obstacles, and many more. In this project, I mainly did the programming as well as leave comments on my code which helped tremendously when troubleshooting problems such as overlapping moles/obstacles, screen errors, and lag. In this project, I learned how hard it is to get the perfect code right away as a few typos or contradictions could ruin and crash your game. This helped me establish a great foundation of leaving comments, troubleshooting code, and patience (the longest bug to find was 2 days). Additionally, this experience taught me the importance of teamwork and communication as a team and that two brains are always better than one. Having more than one person look over code helps gives another perspective on to how to approach a problem. Overall, the project went pretty well and I am happy with how it turned out.
