Solar Hydrogen Vehicle

Duration - October 31, 2019 - November 18, 2019
Course - Principles of Engineering
Objective - To design and construct a vehicle that can travel 1 meter using hydrogen or solar energy
Type of Project - 4 Person Group

For this project, our group was tasked with manipulating images based on the expectations of a fake client. Our client was family, and they wanted to a border and text, and many user options. As this was a relatively simple task, I decided to experiment with different methods to go above and beyond. As a result, I created many different user options that they could do, such as having 2 separate borders (striped diagonally or striped vertically and horizontally), tinting the image , having the user choose a border size, choosing the border color, choosing the text color, and many more. Some of the issues that were faced was simply knowing how the libraries (PIL) worked. However, after doing a lot of research, experimentation, and brainstorming, I managed to get the paste, tint, and user_input() function to work together. I learned that no matter how hard something may seem at first if the will is there, there is a way it can be done. Additionally, I learned the importance of clean and documented code, as it made it significantly easier to troubleshoot issues. Troubleshooting skills were also obtained by adding breakpoints in certain lines of code, in order to see what the different images looked like (border images with a pattern tinted image, resized image, etc). Overall, this was a very fun project and I am proud of what I learned and accomplished.

MIT App Inventor Project

Duration - September 18, 2018 - October 6, 2018
Course - Computer Science Principles
Objective - Implement new features to a certain game using MIT App Inventor
Type of Project - 2 Person Group

For this project, the game that our group worked on was Mole Mash. In the original, unedited version, a mole pops up at a random position on the screen and the player scores points by hitting the mole before it jumps away. My partner and I explored and added bonus features to make the game more intriguing such as a life system, levels, timers, start/instructions screen, a cloud saving score, obstacles, and many more. In this project, I mainly did the programming as well as leave comments on my code which helped tremendously when troubleshooting problems such as overlapping moles/obstacles, screen errors, and lag. In this project, I learned how hard it is to get the perfect code right away as a few typos or contradictions could ruin and crash your game. This helped me establish a great foundation of leaving comments, troubleshooting code, and patience (the longest bug to find was 2 days). Additionally, this experience taught me the importance of teamwork and communication as a team and that two brains are always better than one. Having more than one person look over code helps gives another perspective on to how to approach a problem. Overall, the project went pretty well and I am happy with how it turned out.
