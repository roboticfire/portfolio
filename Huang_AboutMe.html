About Me

Ever since I played my first game, Minecraft Pocket Edition, I was fascinated by the world that showed up on the screen in front of me. Virtual animals that made noises and walked, creepers and zombies that would chase you, being able to destroy blocks and place, building giant mansions and even add code to do certain things — that all fascinated me and introduced me to the infinite possibilities of the internet. Ever since, I have always been wondering about how something happens — the logic behind each action. From games such as Clash Royale and Roblox, as well as watching my dad work behind multiple screens, I knew that engineering is the right choice for me.

The arts and sports outside of school have also captivated me to explore and learn from the world around me. Throughout my life, I have constantly been interested and involved in many different activities. These include choir, drawing lessons, tennis, speech and debate, robotics, competitive swimming for 9+ years, piano for 9+ years, and many more. If there was one thing that I learned from all these activities is that you never learn these skills overnight. Behind every charcoal drawing that I finished, there were 10+ hours of painstaking work behind it. For every race I swam, there were hundreds if not thousands of hours of rigorous training behind it. For countless days I wanted to give up – but I didn’t. Whether it would be sitting down at the piano, jumping in the pool, or stepping up on stage - all those little moments taught me diligence and to never give up.

These traits also carried over to my school career, where I strive to be the best I can every day. To pursue my goal as a software engineering, I have joined many clubs and activities dedicated to engineering. For example, recently, I have joined Gael Force Robotics, a student-run high school club whose members work on both real-life engineering projects and participate in the VEX Robotics Competition. Additionally, I am now a part of DEDA, the Dublin Engineering and Design Academy, where I learn engineering skills through courses such as Principles of Engineering and Computer Science Principles. Through these classes, I have learned engineering skills such as building and designing (POE), as well as programming (CSP). I am currently proficient in Java, Python, HTML, CSS, and Netlogo. Overall, I am a well-rounded student who is always looking forward to learning new things. Thank you for reading and enjoy the rest of my portfolio!
